Mohammed Rafi - A Lifestory

The Golden Voice of India, Forever Echoing in Our Hearts

Mohammed Rafi, born on December 24, 1924, in Kotla Sultan Singh near Amritsar, grew up with a passion for music, nurtured by his brother Hamid. Rafi trained under Ustad Abdul Wahid Khan in Lahore before moving to Bombay in 1944, where he began his illustrious playback singing career. Known for his versatility, Rafi delivered timeless classics across genres, collaborating with legends like Naushad, Shankar-Jaikishan, and R.D. Burman. Despite facing competition from Kishore Kumar in the 1970s, Rafi made a remarkable comeback and was celebrated for his humility, generosity, and deep emotional connection with music. He recorded over 7,000 songs, often sang without charging struggling composers, and remained humble despite his immense fame. His story still inspires singers and shows how music can last and touch people's hearts.

Birth

Mohammed Rafi was born on December 24, 1924, in Kotla Sultan Singh, Punjab (now Majitha, Amritsar) to parents Haji Ali Mohammad and Allah Rakhi. He was born into a Punjabi Jat Muslim family.

Marriage

Mohammed Rafi married twice. His first marriage was to his cousin, Bashira Bibi, in his ancestral village. However, the marriage ended when Bashira moved to Lahore, Pakistan, following the killing of her parents during the Partition riots. Rafi's second marriage was to Bilquis Bano in 1943. Mohammed Rafi's children from his first marriage to Bashira Bibi include his son, Saeed Rafi, while his children from his second marriage to Bilquis Bano are Shahid Rafi, Khalid Rafi, Zubeda Rafi, and Nashreen Ahmed.
